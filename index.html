So You Think You Want a Dog

If you are thinking about getting a dog, there are several things to consider before actually getting one.

  1. Why do you want a dog?
  2. Do you have the time, money, living situation, etc to accommodate a dog of whatever size/energy level you choose?
  3. What type of dog is right for your family/living situation?

Why do you want a dog?

Answer that question honestly. Why do you really want a dog? Do you want a dog to perform a specific service for you (i.e. protection, guard farm animals, therapy, etc.)? Do you want one to get your kids to shut up about wanting a dog? Or is because you want to add a new family member? Whatever, your reason for wanting one, the biggest thing you need to look at is are you going to be able to provide a warm, loving home for the entirety of the dog's lifespan? If you answered no to that question, you need to consider just getting a stuffed animal.

Do you have the means to accommodate a dog?

Dogs need four main things to keep them happy and healthy. If you are are not prepared to provide all of these things for the 12+ years your dog will be alive, then you need to seriously reconsider getting a dog.

  • Sustainence(food, water, treats, etc): dog food can get expensive, especially if your dog develops food allergies. Make sure you know what you are getting into financially.
  • Shelter: at the absolute very least, your dog will need some kind of insulated shelter to protect it from the elements.
  • Veterinary care: just like people need regular medical checkups, dogs need regular veterinary checkups. They will need yearly vaccinations and other preventatives to keep them healthy.
  • Companionship: For generations, dogs have been bred to be companion animals. They crave our attention and aim to please us in any way possible. That means time is going to be a major committment if you decide to get a dog.

What type of dog is right for your family?

The type of dog you choose needs to depend on your living situation, how active you are, and whether you have kids or other animals already. More active dog breeds will require a more active family. Some dog breeds don't do well around kids or small animals. Some need more room to roam. Whatever breed you decide is best, always consider adopting from a reputable rescue!
